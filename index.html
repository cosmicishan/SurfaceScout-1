Classification

Lunar Surface

We developed a model to classify mars satelite images from the HiRISE dataset captured by the Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter. The model will categorize various Martian surface features, including different types of dunes, craters, impact ejecta, and unique geological formations.

Detection

Detection of mars rock

The Yolo model that's been trained to detect the Rock on the mask. It will draw the bounding box on the Rock and display the new image with the bounding box. It will display box of all the rocks exists on the image.

References

Dataset

The dataset is from The NASA Planetary Data System who hosts millions of images acquired from the planet Mars. To help users quickly find images of interest, they have developed and deployed content-based classification and search capabilities for Mars orbital and surface images.

Open dataset

Research Paper

In research paper they have created two new labeled data sets to train and evaluate the latest versions of our Mars image classifiers. The HiRISE images were collected by the High Resolution Imaging Experiment (HiRISE) instrument onboard the Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter (MRO) (McEwen et al. 2007).
